James Brown: Early Life and Education

James was born to John Brown and Maryann Brown. He was born on 25th February 1951 and raised in Washington D.C., United States.

American Sportscaster is the eldest brother to one sister and three brothers.

He was born into a working-class family. Maryann was a homemaker, while John used to do two jobs to support his family.

Despite all the hardship, Jame’s father and mother made a great effort to provide their children a good life. James’s parents even supported his love for sports.

J.B. attended DeMatha Catholic Highschool, and then he graduated from Harvard University.

Brown always shined brighter among other players on the basketball court. Similarly, in his last three seasons at Harvard University, he achieved all Ivy-League honors.

He also played the role of team captain in his senior year at Harvard.

At Harvard, Cornel Ronald West, who is an American Philosopher, was his roommate.

James Brown: Career

Brown’s Basketball Career

James was fond of basketball and baseball. In his high school days, Morgan Wotten coached him, and James developed basketball skills more than baseball.

As Sportscaster

The year 1948 was the beginning of his sportscasting career. He served as an expert commenter on NBA Washington Bullets for Home team Sports. J.B. was sports repoter in Channel 9.

He also worked as an analyst of the NBA at CBS network. Besides, he was also an anchor at CBS affiliated with WUSA.

He also worked for Fox Network for a short period. James was the host for NFL on Fox in 1994. Along with that, Brown also worked as studio host for Fox Saturday Baseball and Fox NHL Saturday.

He ended his sportscasting career in Fox Network in 2006. And, then he again rejoined the CBS network.

He appeared as a broadcaster along with Boomer Esiason, Shannon Sharpe and, Dan Marino in NFL today, which aired on the CBS network.
