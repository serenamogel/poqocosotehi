How did Phil Murphy start his Professional Career?

Biography After graduating, he started his career in 1982 with a summer associate position at Goldman Sachs. From 1993 to 1997, Philip was in charge of managing Goldman Sachs’ business in Germany, Switzerland, and Austria. In addition, from 1997 to 1999, he oversaw the Asian branch. He was also added to the company’s Management Committee in 1999.

He became a global co-head of the company’s investment management division in 2001. In the EMEA area, he also played a key role in the creation of a department devoted to emerging markets. In 2003, he was promoted to Senior Director and relieved of his day-to-day duties at the company. He left in 2005–2006. He worked for Goldman Sachs for 23 years.

Philip aided in funding his close friend Howard Dean’s “50 state plan” while serving as National Finance Chair of the Democratic National Committee from 2006 to 2009.

He was appointed ambassador to Germany by President Barack Obama in 2009, and upon learning that diplomatic cables had been released showing him insulting Chancellor Merkel and Guido Westerwelle, he resigned in 2013.

He eventually established his business management consulting firm and the liberal think group New Start New Jersey, and in May 2016, an unusual time for announcements, he declared his candidacy for governor of New Jersey.

Notwithstanding John S. Wisniewski’s criticism of Murphy as an “establishment” Goldman Sachs banker, Murphy raced to a commanding lead in the Democratic primary election after Jersey City Mayor Steven Fulop endorsed him.

He won the election by defeating Kim Guadagno, the Republican Party’s nominee. Sheila Oliver is the second woman and the first African-American to hold the role of Lieutenant Governor after him.

During the COVID-19 outbreak, he was governor of New Jersey, overseeing the state’s careful, gradual reopening after months of lockdown and mask restrictions.

In his progressive reelection campaign in 2021, he praised New Jersey’s tenacity in the face of the COVID pandemic. By encouraging mask use during COVID and implementing COVID policies that many Republicans believed were harmful to small businesses, Murphy was accused by Trump supporters of breaching citizens’ rights.
