Jeremy Mayfield Biography and Wiki

Jeremy Mayfield is an American stock car racing driver who was born in Owensboro, Kentucky, United States. He drove cars for the Sadler brothers, Michael Kranefuss, Roger Penske, T.W. Taylor, Cale Yarborough, Ray Evernham, Bill Davis, and Gene Haas. In 2009, Mayfield drove for Mayfield Motorsports which is his own team. Currently, he competes at local dirt tracks around the northwest of the state of Georgia.

He also drove for Michael Kranefuss, Roger Penske, Ray Evernham, Bill Davis, and Gene Haas. Mayfield started racing in his native Owensboro, Kentucky, hustling BMX bikes. Before long, he got down to business for Sadler Siblings Hustling as a fabricator before turning into their driver and winning Late Model Youngster of the Year at Kentucky Engine Speedway in 1987. In 2012 he paid a fine of $1 million because his dog made scratches on the delivery man.

He started the 2001 season with two back-to-back third-place finishes but was, however, discharged after the Security One 400. Jeremy then drove for the Penske Racing South team. He ran 20 begins in his debut season, his best completion a nineteenth at Rockingham.

In 1995, Jeremy raced for Yarborough full-time and had an eighth-place keep running at the Mill operator Certified Draft 500, with a 31st-place complete in the focus standings in the wake of meeting all requirements for 27 out of 31 races.

Jeremy Mayfield House

In December 2013, a million-dollar mansion once owned by Mayfield was burned to the ground during planned firefighter training. The house was once reportedly valued at $1.8 million. Everything in the seven-bath house, seven-bedroom was stripped down from the windows to the wiring. Mayfield was evicted from the property in 2012 after financial troubles stemming from a failed drug test in 2009 that banned him from racing.

Neighbors Chris Shook and his mother Crystal Shook live across the street and watched as it went down. Mayfield bought the home and surrounding property for $3.8 million in 2006. Jeremy Mayfield 2019

It’s been over nine years since Jeremy Mayfield was banned from NASCAR after testing positive for meth. The controversy ruined his career in the sport; despite Mayfield’s repeated denials, some were left thinking the driver was an addict. But now, new occurrences have those people starting to believe his side of the story. Once news broke Aug. 6, 2019 that NASCAR CEO and Chairman Brian France was arrested for driving under the influence and possession of Oxycodone; social media was flooded with Mayfield supporters.

It took nine years to prove it, but it turns out Mayfield’s claims were right. The powerful leader who banned him for failing a drug test was using drugs himself. Mayfield feels he has been proven right on a number of other accounts as well. But when the news broke about France, Mayfield didn’t respond with joy or attack France’s character.
