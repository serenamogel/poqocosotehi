Devin Haney is an American professional boxer who is the current undisputed lightweight champion. He has also held the WBC title since April 2020, and the WBA, WBO, IBF, and The Ring titles since June 2022. As of May 2022, he is ranked as the world’s fourth-best active lightweight by The Ring magazine, Transnational Boxing Rankings Board, BoxRec, and ESPN. As an amateur, Haney won seven National titles. He turned pro at the age of 17 but he couldn’t compete in the United States due to age regulations and competed in Mexico for his first 4 matches. His stance is orthodox. He is nicknamed ‘The Dream’.

Devin Haney Career Timeline

  • Haney won seven National titles during his amateur career. 
  • In January 2015, at the age of 17, he became the youngest boxer to win the Youth World Championships in Reno. He had an amateur record of 138–8 bouts before turning professional in 2015 at the age of 17. 
  • He turned pro at the age of 17 and fight the first four matches in Mexico. 
  • He won the WBC Youth, IBF-USBA, IBF North American, WBC International, WBO Inter-Continental, and WBA International lightweight titles as a professional.
  • On 2nd February 2018, he was scheduled to fight Filipino Harmonito Dela Torre in an eight-round opening bout. 
  • On 11th January 2019, he dominated a very tough opponent in Xolisani Ndongeni on the way to a 10-round unanimous decision win. 
  • In April 2019, he signed a co-promotional deal between his own company, Devin Haney Promotions, and British promoter Eddie Hearn’s Matchroom Boxing.
  • On 25th May 2019, he defeated Antonio Moran via a vicious seventh-round knockout, in his first match under the Matchroom banner.
  • On 13th September 2019, he defeated Zaur Abdullaev (11–0) to win the vacant WBC interim lightweight title. 
  • In addition to this, he became the youngest (i.e. latest-born) world champion in boxing when he was elevated as the WBC lightweight champion after previous WBC lightweight champion Vasiliy Lomachenko was promoted to “Franchise Champion” by the WBC in October 2019. 
  • After that, he fought against Alfredo Santiago, Yuriorkis Gamboa, Jorge Linares, and Joseph Diaz. 
  • Recently, he defeated George Kambosos by outpointing the Australian fighter in their Melbourne Australia fight in June 2022 to become the undisputed world lightweight boxing champion. The judges scored the bout 116-112, 116-112, 118-110, all in favor of Haney.
