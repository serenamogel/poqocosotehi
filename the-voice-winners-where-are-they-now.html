The Voice Winners Where Are They Now?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, February 26, 2024

The Voice, NBC’s hit singing competition, has been captivating audiences since its inception in 2011. With its unique format of blind auditions and celebrity coaching, the show has not only provided entertainment but also launched the careers of many aspiring singers. However, the question that often lingers in the minds of fans is: What happens to the winners after the show? This article delves into the post-show journeys of The Voice winners, exploring where they are now and how their careers have unfolded.


SeasonWinnerCoachPost-Show Highlights

Season 1: Javier Colon

Javier Colon, the inaugural winner of The Voice, was coached by Adam Levine. After his victory, Colon released several albums, including “Come Through For You” shortly after the show. Despite parting ways with Universal Republic Records, he has continued to make music independently and performs regularly.

Season 2: Jermaine Paul

Jermaine Paul, a former backup singer for Alicia Keys, won the second season under Blake Shelton’s mentorship. He released his single “I Believe in This Life” and continues to be active in the music industry, though he has yet to achieve mainstream success.

Season 3: Cassadee Pope

Cassadee Pope, the first female winner of The Voice, has arguably become one of the most successful contestants. Coached by Blake Shelton, she embraced her country roots and released her debut album “Frame by Frame,” which topped the country charts. Pope continues to record music and tour, solidifying her place in the country music scene.

Season 4: Danielle Bradbery

At just 16, Danielle Bradbery won Season 4 and has since been making waves in country music. Her self-titled debut album featured the hit single “The Heart of Dixie.” Bradbery has released multiple albums and remains a promising young talent in the industry.

Season 5: Tessanne Chin

Tessanne Chin’s powerful voice led her to victory in Season 5. The Jamaican recording artist released her album “Count on My Love” and continues to perform and release music, enjoying a successful career, particularly in the Caribbean.

Season 6: Josh Kaufman

Under Usher’s guidance, Josh Kaufman won Season 6. Post-The Voice, Kaufman starred in the Broadway production of “Pippin” and continues to pursue music and live performances.

Season 7: Craig Wayne Boyd

Craig Wayne Boyd’s country charm won over audiences in Season 7. Coached by Blake Shelton, Boyd released his album “Top Shelf” and continues to tour and perform across the country.

Season 8: Sawyer Fredericks

At just 16, folk singer-songwriter Sawyer Fredericks won Season 8. He has since released several albums, including “A Good Storm,” and maintains a dedicated fan base while touring extensively.

Season 9: Jordan Smith

Jordan Smith, known for his incredible range, won Season 9. He has released multiple albums, including “Only Love,” and has performed at various high-profile events, including the Presidential Inauguration.

Season 10: Alisan Porter

Alisan Porter, a former child star, took home the win in Season 10. She has released music independently and continues to be active in the music industry, focusing on her solo career.

Season 11: Sundance Head

Sundance Head, with his soulful country voice, won Season 11. He has released music post-The Voice and continues to perform, though he has faced challenges in the industry.

Season 12: Chris Blue

Chris Blue, the winner of Season 12, has released singles like “Blue Blood Blues” and continues to work on his music while engaging in philanthropic efforts.

Season 13: Chloe Kohanski

Chloe Kohanski, now known as Chloe MK, won Season 13 and has since been exploring her sound, releasing alternative pop music and rebranding herself as an indie artist.

Season 14: Brynn Cartelli

At 15, Brynn Cartelli became the youngest winner of The Voice. Coached by Kelly Clarkson, Cartelli has released singles like “Long Way Home” and is working on her debut album.

Season 15: Chevel Shepherd

Chevel Shepherd’s country roots led her to victory in Season 15. She has released singles post-The Voice and is working on her debut EP while finishing high school.

Season 16: Maelyn Jarmon

Maelyn Jarmon’s stunning performances won her Season 16. She has released her single “Wait for You” and continues to work on new music.

Season 17: Jake Hoot

Jake Hoot’s country sound won him Season 17. He has released his EP “Love Out of Time” and continues to build his career in country music.

Season 18: Todd Tilghman

Todd Tilghman, a pastor turned singer, won Season 18. He has been working on his music career while balancing family life and his pastoral duties.

Season 19: Carter Rubin

Carter Rubin, another young talent, won Season 19. He is currently working on his music while balancing his education.

Season 20: Cam Anthony

Cam Anthony’s exceptional talent led him to victory in Season 20. He is currently working on his music career and has a promising future ahead.

FAQ Section

  • Who is the most successful winner of The Voice?
    Cassadee Pope from Season 3 is considered one of the most successful winners, with a thriving career in country music.
  • Do winners of The Voice get a record deal?
    Yes, winners of The Voice typically receive a record deal as part of their prize package, along with a cash prize.
  • How many winners of The Voice have achieved mainstream success?
    While several winners have enjoyed success, particularly in niche markets like country music, mainstream success varies and is not guaranteed.

In conclusion, the winners of The Voice have taken diverse paths in their music careers. Some have found considerable success, while others continue to work towards their big break. The show has undoubtedly provided a platform for these artists to showcase their talents, but it is their perseverance and dedication that determine their path post-The Voice. As the show continues to produce new talent, fans eagerly watch to see how each winner will use the opportunity to shape their musical destiny.
